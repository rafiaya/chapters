Chapter 3: Introduction to Telescopes

Types of Telescopes

Telescopes come in several types, each designed for a specific purpose. The three main types are optical telescopes, radio telescopes, and space telescopes. Each type allows us to observe different aspects of the universe, from visible light to radio waves.

Optical Telescopes

Optical telescopes, such as refractors and reflectors, collect and magnify visible light. These are the most common telescopes and have been crucial in the development of modern astronomy.

Radio Telescopes

Radio telescopes collect and study radio waves emitted by distant celestial objects. These telescopes are crucial for studying the universe in wavelengths that optical telescopes cannot detect.

Chapter 2: Early Space Exploration

Space Race

The Space Race was a period of intense competition between the United States and the Soviet Union to dominate space exploration. This race was a direct result of Cold War tensions and became a key part of the rivalry between the two superpowers.

The Beginning of Space Exploration

In 1957, the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, into orbit, marking the beginning of the space age. The U.S. responded by creating NASA in 1958 and launching its own satellites and human space programs.

The Apollo Program

The Apollo Program was the United States' most ambitious space initiative, aiming to land a man on the Moon. On July 20, 1969, the successful landing of Apollo 11 resulted in the first humans, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin, walking on the Moon.

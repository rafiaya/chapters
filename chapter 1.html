Chapter 1: Introduction to NASA

What is NASA?

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) is the United States government agency responsible for space exploration, aeronautics research, and scientific discovery. It was established on July 29, 1958, replacing the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA).

History of NASA

NASA was created during the Cold War space race, primarily in response to the Soviet Union’s launch of Sputnik 1 in 1957—the world’s first artificial satellite. Since then, NASA has led the U.S. in space missions, pioneering both human and robotic exploration.

Key Achievements

  • Apollo Program (1969–1972): Successfully landed humans on the Moon, starting with Apollo 11 in 1969.
  • Space Shuttle Program (1981–2011): Developed reusable spacecraft, enabling construction of the International Space Station (ISS).
  • Hubble Space Telescope (1990): Revolutionized our understanding of the universe with deep-space imagery.

Current Missions

Today, NASA is focused on Artemis, a program to return humans to the Moon and prepare for future Mars missions. The agency also continues to operate the International Space Station, monitor Earth’s climate, and send robotic missions to explore distant planets and asteroids.

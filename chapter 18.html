Chapter 18: Hubble Space Telescope and Beyond

Introduction

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST), launched in 1990, revolutionized astronomy by providing clear, detailed images of the universe from above Earth’s atmosphere. It has helped us understand galaxies, stars, black holes, and the age of the universe.

The Power of Hubble

  • Captured iconic images like the Pillars of Creation in the Eagle Nebula.
  • Measured the rate of expansion of the universe (Hubble constant).
  • Discovered evidence of dark energy accelerating cosmic expansion.
  • Studied the atmospheres of distant exoplanets.

Challenges and Repairs

Hubble faced problems early on due to a flawed main mirror. However, astronauts on space shuttle missions repaired and upgraded it, extending its lifespan and keeping it at the forefront of science.

Beyond Hubble: The Next Generation

  • James Webb Space Telescope (JWST): Launched in 2021, it observes in infrared, looking deeper into the universe’s past to study the first galaxies and stars.
  • Roman Space Telescope (planned): Will study dark energy, dark matter, and conduct wide-field surveys of the cosmos.
  • Future Concepts: Telescopes like LUVOIR and HabEx aim to directly image Earth-like exoplanets.

Impact on Humanity

Hubble inspired not only scientists but also the public, bringing breathtaking views of space into homes worldwide. Its discoveries have reshaped our place in the universe.

Conclusion

The Hubble Space Telescope is one of humanity’s greatest scientific instruments. As newer telescopes take its place, Hubble’s legacy will live on in both the science it produced and the wonder it sparked.

Chapter 4: The Hubble Space Telescope

Introduction

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST), launched in 1990, is one of the most important astronomical tools ever created. Unlike ground-based telescopes, Hubble orbits outside Earth’s atmosphere, giving it a clear, unobstructed view of the universe.

Why is Hubble Special?

Earth’s atmosphere blocks or distorts much of the light coming from space. By being above the atmosphere, Hubble can capture sharp, high-resolution images across ultraviolet, visible, and near-infrared wavelengths.

Key Discoveries

  • Measured the age of the universe to be around 13.8 billion years.
  • Provided evidence of dark energy, an unknown force causing the universe’s expansion to accelerate.
  • Captured stunning images of galaxies, nebulae, and planets that changed how we see the cosmos.

Legacy

Hubble has been repaired and upgraded multiple times by astronauts. It remains active today and continues to work alongside newer observatories like the James Webb Space Telescope, ensuring its legacy in astronomical research.

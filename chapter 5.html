Chapter 5: The James Webb Space Telescope

Introduction

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), launched on December 25, 2021, is NASA’s most advanced space observatory. It is designed to look deeper into the universe than ever before, acting as a time machine to study the first galaxies formed after the Big Bang.

Design and Features

Unlike Hubble, JWST observes mainly in the infrared spectrum, allowing it to see through cosmic dust clouds and detect faint light from distant galaxies. Its massive 6.5-meter gold-coated mirror collects much more light than Hubble’s 2.4-meter mirror.

Key Goals

  • Study the formation of the first stars and galaxies.
  • Examine exoplanets and their atmospheres for signs of habitability.
  • Understand the origins of galaxies, stars, and planetary systems.
